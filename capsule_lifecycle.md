
# DCE Capsule Lifecycle — Canonical Definition (v1.0)

## 1. Lock‑In Phase

The Lock‑In Phase is the moment a decision’s intent, rationale, and constraints are captured before decay begins. This phase freezes the original meaning of the decision so that it can be preserved and referenced later.

**Purpose**  
- Prevent early drift and ambiguity.  
- Capture the “original state” of the decision before it is influenced by time, emotion, or external pressure.

**Artifacts**  
- Intent statement (what this decision is trying to achieve).  
- Constraints (what must or must not happen).  
- Rationale (why this decision exists).  
- Expected trajectory (how this decision is supposed to unfold over time).

---

## 2. Capsule Phase

In the Capsule Phase, the decision is sealed into a Continuity Capsule — a structured container that preserves its identity across time and context shifts.

**Properties**  
- Immutable intent: the original intent cannot be silently altered.  
- Traceable lineage: any change must be traceable back to the original.  
- Context‑independent preservation: the decision can survive environment changes.  
- Authority inheritance rules: it is clear who can extend, modify, or branch from it.

**Function**  
- Protect the decision from decay, drift, or reinterpretation.  
- Ensure that any future reference to the decision can be compared against the original.

---

## 3. Seed Phase

In the Seed Phase, the capsule is opened and used to generate new decisions that inherit the original intent. The capsule becomes a “seed source” for downstream decisions.

**Characteristics**  
- Intent propagation: new decisions inherit the core intent of the original.  
- Constraint inheritance: key constraints are preserved unless explicitly overridden.  
- Controlled divergence: changes are allowed, but they are explicit and traceable.  
- Decision branching: multiple new decisions can grow from the same capsule.

**Purpose**  
- Ensure continuity across evolving contexts.  
- Allow the system (or person) to adapt while still honoring the original intent.

---

## 4. Root Phase

In the Root Phase, the decision becomes part of the Continuity Substrate — the deeper layer of stable decisions, principles, or commitments that influence future decisions.

**Properties**  
- Long‑term stability: the decision is no longer “temporary” but foundational.  
- Cross‑decision influence: it shapes how future decisions are evaluated and made.  
- Integration into identity or system logic: it becomes part of “how this system thinks.”

**Purpose**  
- Establish a stable, enduring decision architecture.  
- Allow DCE systems (or humans using DCE) to build a coherent, non‑fragile decision history.

---

## Summary

The DCE Capsule Lifecycle describes how a decision moves through four phases:

1. **Lock‑In** — capture the original intent before decay.  
2. **Capsule** — preserve the decision in a protected container.  
3. **Seed** — use the capsule to generate new, inheriting decisions.  
4. **Root** — integrate the decision into the long‑term continuity substrate.

This lifecycle is a core mechanism of Decision Continuity Engineering (DCE), ensuring that decisions are not isolated events but continuity objects that can survive time, change, and context shifts without losing their original meaning.
